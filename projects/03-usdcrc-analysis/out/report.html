USD/CRC · MONEX — the calendar strategy

2,663 sessions · 2014-12-08 → 2026-06-19 · long/short USD · $1.00M per trade · priced VWAP-to-VWAP, net of 0.65 CRC round-trip · no technical indicators

One tradeable edge: Costa Rica's mid-month payment calendar. Firms sell USD to raise colones for the 15th IVA (D-104) tax filing and the quincena payroll, so the colón strengthens into the deadline and reverts after. The rule is short USD within 6 business days of the deadline, long the rest of the month, at half size whenever a slow 20-day volume regime disagrees. Selection is disciplined — variants are ranked in-sample (first 60% of history) and every headline here is the out-of-sample realisation on the 40% held out after 2021-11-18. On that held-out window the rule earns $123k/yr at Sharpe 3.55 (full-sample Sharpe 3.31, ~18 trades/yr), and it strengthens out of sample (3.22 → 3.55) where the flat-sized entries decay. It is the only edge in the study that works in both the pegged 2014–2021 regime and the 2021–2026 float.

§1 · Why it works — the cash-flow cycle and the BCCR's hand

1. The mechanism, in one picture

Re-index the price series from raw day-of-month to business-days-to-the-deadline and the mechanism is unmistakable. Private firms convert USD→CRC for the 15th, a recurring supply surge that strengthens the colón into the deadline (−9 to −16 bps next-day) and reverts after. The BCCR is not a bystander: official flow — the central bank plus the non-bank public sector (RECOPE et al.) routed through MONEX — is a median 52% of daily volume, and official USD demand peaks exactly at the turn. Heavy official demand does not push USD up; it marks the point where private mid-month supply overwhelms (contemporaneous corr -0.25). So the calendar reversion and the official footprint are two views of one cash-flow cycle.

§2 · The rule & what each layer earns

2. Built one design choice at a time

Exact logic

≤ 6 business days before the IVA (D-104) / quincena deadline (through it) → SHORT USD $1M
   (firms sell USD to raise colones for the 15th tax filing + payroll → colón strengthens)
otherwise → LONG USD $1M (supply fades, USD drifts back up)
size trim: hold HALF size whenever a slow 20-day volume regime disagrees with the trade
optional exit: bank the trade once it gives back 30 bps from its best (hard floor 40 bps)

Each row adds ONE choice on top of the row above. IS Sharpe is the selection metric (first 60%); OOS Sharpe is the honest confirmation on the 40% held out after 2021-11-18 and never used to choose. The recommended always-in-market rule is ② Calendar + slow-vol; the exit and reserve layers (③–④) are optional risk improvements, not more alpha.

Read the layers as risk, not money. The slow-vol trim (②) is what makes it trade-ready — it holds or improves out of sample while flat-sized entries decay, turning out-of-sample per-trade skew positive (+0.74) and halving the worst trade ($-50k → $-18k). The exit overlay (③) is a second, independent tail control: a paired t-test on daily OOS P&L gives p = 0.7292 — total P&L is statistically unchanged, but max drawdown drops ~45% and OOS Sharpe rises +1.13. A take-profit actively hurts (the edge is a drift into the deadline) and is excluded. The reserve trim (④) beats a blind "halve every long" control by $56k out of sample (paired t = 3.31, p = 0.001) — the reserve regime picks which longs to cut.

  • The flow signals are regime-contingent. Order-flow, volume, and skew are worthless-to-negative on the pegged 2014–2021 window and only fire on the 2021–2026 float, so their full-sample numbers average a dead regime with a live one — and they carry $-397k-class in-sample drawdowns at 80–97 round trips/yr. Their value here is only as the slow filter that sizes the calendar trade.
  • Honest caveat. Because in-sample is the pegged/low-vol regime and out-of-sample is the float, in-sample selection structurally favours whatever works in both. The calendar rule wins partly because it is that edge — a real virtue, but not the same claim as "the flow signals never work."
§4 · Is it overfit? One look

4. A plateau, a clean held-out split, and passed controls

  • Held out, the edge grows. The recommended rule goes IS 3.22 → OOS 3.55; the exit overlay IS 3.7 → OOS 3.92 — no decay. Across the exit parameter grid corr(IS, OOS) = +0.82, so in-sample ranking genuinely predicts out-of-sample, and the deflated Sharpe (Bailey / López de Prado) over 391 trials gives P(true>0) ≈ 1.0.
  • Positive every year. The rule is positive in all 12 calendar years (worst $34k), including the 2015–17 pegged years where the volume signal was dead. Priced at the closing print instead of the VWAP the edge slightly improves — it is an economic flow, not an artifact of marking to the close.
§5 · Verdict & risks
  • Trade the calendar + slow-vol rule. Short USD into Costa Rica's mid-month IVA / payroll deadline (within 6 business days), long the rest of the month, at half size when a slow 20-day volume regime disagrees. Full-sample $95k/yr at Sharpe 3.31, ~18 trades/yr — and, the number that matters, out-of-sample Sharpe 3.55 ($123k/yr on the 40% held out).
  • The optional overlays are risk, not alpha. A trailing 30 / hard-floor 40 exit tops the ranking (IS 3.7 → OOS 3.92) and roughly halves drawdown, and a BCCR reserve-regime long-trim lifts OOS Sharpe to 4.09 — both smoother paths to size up, with total P&L statistically unchanged. Add them if you want the drawdown, skip them for simplicity.
  • The mechanism is economic, not technical: exporter USD supply and the tax/payroll cycle. That is why it persists across the peg and the float — and why the dominant, unpriceable risk is a re-peg or heavy BCCR intervention: the bank is already ~half of MONEX and could mute the edge at will. The reserve trim is a partial hedge, not an escape.
  • Limits. The float that carries most of the edge is only the last ~4.5 years; results are net of VWAP slippage but gross of financing/borrow and market impact; the colón is not freely shortable for all participants — this is a directional/treasury edge, not a market-neutral one.
